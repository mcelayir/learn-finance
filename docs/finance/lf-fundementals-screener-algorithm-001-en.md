# Fundamental Analysis Stock Screener — Technical Documentation

## Overview

This document specifies a **15-condition stock screening formula** designed to identify fundamentally strong, growing, and financially healthy companies listed on **Borsa İstanbul (BIST)** — Turkey's national stock exchange.

The screener is intended to be implemented on **TradingView** or a compatible financial data platform. A stock must satisfy **all 15 conditions simultaneously** to pass the filter.

This document is written for engineers and AI assistants who may have no prior finance background. Every term is defined, every formula is spelled out, and every condition is stated precisely.

> **Note on Turkish terminology:** The original criteria were defined in Turkish. Turkish terms are included in parentheses throughout this document for reference only, in case you encounter them in data sources or TradingView fields targeting the Turkish market.

---

## Glossary of Financial Terms

All terms used in the 15 criteria are defined here. Read this section first before implementing anything.

| Term | Definition | Formula |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Revenue** (Satışlar) | The total money a company earns from selling its products or services. Also called "Sales" or "Turnover". | — |
| **Net Profit** (Net Kar) | What remains after subtracting all costs, operating expenses, interest, and taxes from revenue. The "bottom line". | Revenue − All Expenses − Taxes − Interest |
| **EBITDA** (Favök) | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A measure of core operational profitability that strips out financing and accounting effects. | Operating Profit + Depreciation + Amortization |
| **EBITDA Margin** (Favök Marjı) | EBITDA expressed as a percentage of Revenue. Shows how much of each unit of revenue becomes operational profit. | EBITDA / Revenue |
| **Shareholders' Equity** (Özkaynak) | The net value of the company owned by shareholders. Equal to total assets minus total liabilities. Also called "Book Value". | Total Assets − Total Liabilities |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** (Özkaynak Karlılığı) | Measures how efficiently the company generates profit from shareholders' equity. | Net Profit / Shareholders' Equity |
| **Operating Profit** (Esas Faaliyet Karı) | Profit generated from core business operations before interest and taxes. Also known as EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes). | Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold − Operating Expenses |
| **Earnings Per Share (EPS)** (Hisse Başına Kar) | The portion of net profit allocated to each outstanding share. | Net Profit / Total Shares Outstanding |
| **Free Float Rate** (Fiili Dolaşım Oranı) | The percentage of a company's total shares that are freely available for public trading (i.e. not locked up by founders, insiders, or strategic shareholders). | Publicly Traded Shares / Total Shares |
| **P/E Ratio** | Price-to-Earnings Ratio. The stock's current market price divided by its earnings per share. Indicates how much investors pay per unit of profit. | Share Price / EPS |
| **PEG Ratio** (PEG Rasyosu) | Price/Earnings-to-Growth Ratio. Adjusts the P/E ratio by the company's earnings growth rate to assess whether the valuation is justified by growth. | P/E Ratio / Annual Earnings Growth Rate (%) |
| **Current Ratio** (Cari Oran) | Measures the company's ability to pay its short-term obligations using its short-term assets. | Current Assets / Current Liabilities |
| **Current Assets** | Assets the company expects to convert to cash within one year (cash, receivables, inventory, etc.). | — |
| **Current Liabilities** | Obligations the company must pay within one year (payables, short-term debt, etc.). | — |
| **Net Debt** (Net Borç) | The company's total debt minus its cash holdings. Represents the true debt burden — a negative value means the company holds more cash than debt. | Total Debt − Cash & Cash Equivalents |
| **Cash Ratio** (Nakit Oran) | The strictest liquidity measure. Shows how much of current liabilities can be covered using only cash on hand. | Cash & Cash Equivalents / Current Liabilities |
| **Quick Ratio / Liquidity Ratio** (Likidite Oranı) | Like the Current Ratio but excludes inventory, since inventory is less easily converted to cash. | (Current Assets − Inventory) / Current Liabilities |
| **Operating Cash Flow** (İşletme Faaliyetleri Nakit Akımı) | The actual cash generated by the company's core business operations in a given period. More reliable than net profit because it cannot be easily manipulated by accounting choices. | Found directly on the Cash Flow Statement |

## The 15 Screening Criteria

---

### Criterion 1 — Star Market Listing
**Turkish name:** Yıldız Pazar

**What it means:** The stock must be listed on the **Star Market** segment of Borsa İstanbul (BIST). The Star Market is the premium tier of the Turkish stock exchange, reserved for larger and more established companies that meet higher transparency and governance standards.

**Condition:** `market_segment = "YILDIZ"`

**Implementation note:** This is a categorical filter, not a numeric calculation. In TradingView or a BIST data API, look for a field indicating the market segment or listing board. The value to match is typically `"YLDIZ"` or `"YILDIZ"`.

---

### Criterion 2 — Quarterly Increasing Return on Equity (ROE)
**Turkish name:** Çeyreklikte Özkaynak Karlılığını Artıran

**What it means:** The company's ROE must be higher in the most recent quarter than in the quarter before it.

**Formula:**
```
ROE = Net Profit / Shareholders' Equity
```

**Condition:** `ROE(most recent quarter) > ROE(previous quarter)`

**Why it matters:** Rising ROE means the company is becoming more efficient at generating profit from shareholder capital — a sign of improving management quality and business performance.

---

### Criterion 3 — Quarterly Increasing Net Profit
**Turkish name:** Çeyreklikte Net Karını Artıran

**What it means:** The company's Net Profit must be higher in the most recent quarter than in the quarter before it.

**Condition:** `NetProfit(most recent quarter) > NetProfit(previous quarter)`

**Why it matters:** Consistent net profit growth is a basic signal of business health and momentum.

---

### Criterion 4 — Quarterly Increasing Revenue
**Turkish name:** Çeyreklikte Satışlarını Artıran

**What it means:** The company's Revenue (Sales) must be higher in the most recent quarter than in the quarter before it.

**Condition:** `Revenue(most recent quarter) > Revenue(previous quarter)`

**Why it matters:** Revenue growth means the company is expanding — either selling more, reaching more customers, or raising prices successfully.

---

### Criterion 5 — Quarterly Increasing EBITDA
**Turkish name:** Çeyreklikte Favökünü Artıran

**What it means:** The company's EBITDA must be higher in the most recent quarter than in the quarter before it.

**Condition:** `EBITDA(most recent quarter) > EBITDA(previous quarter)`

**Why it matters:** EBITDA growth shows that core operational profitability is improving, independent of interest expenses, taxes, or depreciation schedules.

---

### Criterion 6 — Quarterly Increasing EBITDA Margin
**Turkish name:** Çeyreklikte Favök Marjını Artıran

**What it means:** The company's EBITDA Margin must be higher in the most recent quarter than in the quarter before it.

**Formula:**
```
EBITDA Margin = EBITDA / Revenue
```

**Condition:** `EBITDAMargin(most recent quarter) > EBITDAMargin(previous quarter)`

**Why it matters:** An improving margin means the company is becoming more profitable *relative to its revenue* — it is either growing faster than its costs, or controlling costs better. This is a stronger signal than raw EBITDA growth alone.

---

### Criterion 7 — Quarterly Increasing Operating Profit
**Turkish name:** Çeyreklikte Esas Faaliyet Karını Artıran

**What it means:** The company's Operating Profit (EBIT) must be higher in the most recent quarter than in the quarter before it.

**Condition:** `OperatingProfit(most recent quarter) > OperatingProfit(previous quarter)`

**Why it matters:** Operating profit isolates the core business performance from financing decisions (debt) and tax strategy. Growth here confirms the underlying business is strengthening.

---

### Criterion 8 — Quarterly Increasing Earnings Per Share (EPS)
**Turkish name:** Çeyreklikte Hisse Başına Karını Artıran

**What it means:** The company's EPS must be higher in the most recent quarter than in the quarter before it.

**Formula:**
```
EPS = Net Profit / Total Shares Outstanding
```

**Condition:** `EPS(most recent quarter) > EPS(previous quarter)`

**Why it matters:** EPS growth directly benefits shareholders. It also accounts for share dilution — if the company issued new shares, net profit might grow while EPS stays flat or falls, which would be a red flag.

---

### Criterion 9 — Free Float Rate Below 70%
**Turkish name:** Fiili Dolaşım Oranı %70'den Az

**What it means:** Less than 70% of the company's total shares are freely traded on the open market.

**Condition:** `FreeFloatRate < 0.70`

**Why it matters:** A lower free float means more shares are held by insiders, founders, or strategic investors — which is generally a sign of strong insider confidence. A very high free float (above 70%) can indicate that those closest to the company have reduced their exposure, which may be a negative signal.

---

### Criterion 10 — PEG Ratio Below 1
**Turkish name:** PEG Rasyosu 1'den Küçük

**What it means:** The PEG Ratio must be less than 1.

**Formula:**
```
PEG = (Share Price / EPS) / Annual Earnings Growth Rate (%)
    = P/E Ratio / Earnings Growth Rate (%)
```

**Condition:** `PEG < 1`

**Why it matters:** A PEG below 1 suggests the stock may be **undervalued relative to its growth**. In other words, you are paying less per unit of earnings growth than the market average. This is a classic value-growth signal popularized by investor Peter Lynch.

**Edge case:** PEG is meaningless when the earnings growth rate is zero or negative. Add a guard in your implementation: only evaluate this condition when `EarningsGrowthRate > 0`.

---

### Criterion 11 — Current Ratio Between 1 and 3
**Turkish name:** Cari Oran 1 ile 3 Arasında

**What it means:** The Current Ratio must be at least 1 and no more than 3.

**Formula:**
```
Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
```

**Condition:** `1 ≤ CurrentRatio ≤ 3`

**Why it matters:**
- **Below 1:** The company cannot cover its short-term obligations with its short-term assets — a liquidity risk.
- **Above 3:** The company is holding too much in idle assets (uninvested cash, excess inventory) — a sign of inefficiency.
- **Between 1 and 3:** The healthy range — the company can meet its obligations without wastefully hoarding assets.

---

### Criterion 12 — Net Debt / EBITDA Below 3
**Turkish name:** Net Borç / Favök 3'den Küçük

**What it means:** The company's Net Debt must be less than 3 times its EBITDA.

**Formula:**
```
Net Debt = Total Debt − Cash & Cash Equivalents
Net Debt / EBITDA = Net Debt / EBITDA
```

**Condition:** `(TotalDebt - Cash) / EBITDA < 3`

**Why it matters:** This ratio answers the question: *"How many years of operating earnings would it take to pay off all debt?"* A value below 3 is generally considered manageable leverage. A value above 3 signals potentially dangerous debt levels that could threaten the company's financial stability.

**Edge case:** If Net Debt is negative (the company has more cash than debt), the ratio is negative and automatically passes this check. You may want to handle this explicitly in your code rather than relying on implicit behavior.

---

### Criterion 13 — Cash Ratio Above 0.2
**Turkish name:** Nakit Oran 0.2'den Büyük

**What it means:** The company must hold at least 0.2 units of cash for every 1 unit of current (short-term) liabilities.

**Formula:**
```
Cash Ratio = Cash & Cash Equivalents / Current Liabilities
```

**Condition:** `CashRatio > 0.20`

**Why it matters:** This is the strictest liquidity test — it only counts actual cash, not receivables or inventory. A cash ratio above 0.2 ensures the company has a minimum cash buffer to absorb unexpected short-term financial shocks.

---

### Criterion 14 — Quick Ratio Between 0.5 and 2
**Turkish name:** Likidite Oranı 0.5 ile 2 Arasında

**What it means:** The Quick Ratio must be at least 0.5 and no more than 2.

**Formula:**
```
Quick Ratio = (Current Assets − Inventory) / Current Liabilities
```

**Condition:** `0.5 ≤ QuickRatio ≤ 2`

**Why it matters:** The Quick Ratio is a more conservative version of the Current Ratio — it excludes inventory because inventory can be slow to sell and convert to cash. The range 0.5–2 avoids both illiquidity (too low) and excessive idle liquid assets (too high). It complements Criterion 11 by providing a stricter view of short-term financial health.

---

### Criterion 15 — Positive Year-over-Year Operating Cash Flow Growth
**Turkish name:** Yıllık İşletme Faaliyetleri Nakit Akım Değişimi 0'dan Büyük

**What it means:** The company's Operating Cash Flow must be higher this year than it was last year.

**Condition:** `OperatingCashFlow(current year) > OperatingCashFlow(previous year)`

**Why it matters:** Operating Cash Flow is considered a more reliable measure of financial health than net profit, because it reflects actual cash moving in and out of the business. Net profit can be inflated by accounting choices (e.g. revenue recognition timing, depreciation methods); Operating Cash Flow cannot. Growing Operating Cash Flow confirms that profits are backed by real cash generation.

You will likely need to fetch the two most recent quarters of financial data and compute the comparison yourself. Consider using a supplementary data provider such as **Financial Modeling Prep (FMP)**, **Polygon.io**, or **Yahoo Finance** for quarterly financial statements.

## Implementation Caveats

**1. Quarterly growth comparison (Criteria 2–8)**
These 7 criteria all require comparing the most recent quarter to the previous quarter. This is a non-trivial data requirement because most screener platforms expose only the latest value, not a historical series. Plan to query quarterly financial statement history for at least the last 2 quarters.

**2. Currency and inflation**
This screener targets Turkish-listed stocks. All monetary values are reported in **Turkish Lira (TRY)**. Turkey has experienced high inflation in recent years, which can cause nominal figures (revenue, profit, EBITDA) to appear to grow even when real business performance is flat. For the growth criteria (2–8), consider whether to adjust for inflation or at minimum be aware of this distortion.

**3. EBITDA Margin must be computed**
EBITDA Margin is not a standard direct field in most APIs. Always compute it as `EBITDA / Revenue` per period before comparing.

**4. PEG Ratio is undefined for negative growth**
If a company's earnings growth rate is zero or negative, the PEG Ratio is either undefined or misleading. Your implementation must guard against this: only evaluate Criterion 10 when the earnings growth rate is strictly positive.

**5. Negative Net Debt is valid**
If a company holds more cash than it owes in debt, Net Debt is negative. A negative value divided by a positive EBITDA gives a negative ratio, which automatically satisfies the `< 3` condition. Decide whether this should be treated as an automatic pass or flagged explicitly.

**6. TTM vs. single-quarter values**
Some TradingView fields use **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** aggregation rather than the most recent single quarter. Be precise about which time window each field uses, especially for growth comparisons.
