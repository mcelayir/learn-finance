# Fundamental Analysis Screener — Documentation

## Overview

This document describes a stock screening formula called **"Temel Analiz Formül"** (Turkish: Fundamental Analysis Formula). It is a set of financial criteria used to filter stocks on a screener platform (e.g. TradingView) to identify high-quality, fundamentally sound companies worth investing in.

The screener checks **15 conditions**. A stock must satisfy all of them to pass the filter. Below, each condition is explained in plain English, with its financial meaning, why it matters, and how it maps to data fields typically available in a stock screener API or platform.

---

## Glossary of Key Terms

Before diving into the criteria, here are the financial terms used throughout:

| Term | Turkish | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Revenue / Sales** | Satışlar | Total money a company earns from selling its products/services |
| **Net Profit** | Net Kar | What's left after all expenses, taxes, and interest are paid |
| **EBITDA** | Favök | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization — a measure of core operational profitability |
| **EBITDA Margin** | Favök Marjı | EBITDA divided by Revenue — shows how efficiently the company generates profit from sales |
| **Equity / Book Value** | Özkaynak | The net assets of the company (assets minus liabilities) — represents shareholders' ownership value |
| **Equity Profitability (ROE)** | Özkaynak Karlılığı | Return on Equity — Net Profit divided by Equity — measures how well the company uses shareholder money |
| **Operating Profit** | Esas Faaliyet Karı | Profit from core business operations, before interest and taxes (similar to EBIT) |
| **EPS** | Hisse Başına Kar | Earnings Per Share — Net Profit divided by number of shares outstanding |
| **Free Float Rate** | Fiili Dolaşım Oranı | The percentage of a company's shares available for public trading (not held by insiders or major shareholders) |
| **PEG Ratio** | PEG Rasyosu | Price/Earnings to Growth Ratio — compares the P/E ratio to the company's earnings growth rate |
| **Current Ratio** | Cari Oran | Current Assets divided by Current Liabilities — measures short-term liquidity |
| **Net Debt** | Net Borç | Total Debt minus Cash — represents the company's true debt burden |
| **Cash Ratio** | Nakit Oran | Cash and Cash Equivalents divided by Current Liabilities — a strict liquidity measure |
| **Liquidity Ratio** | Likidite Oranı | Similar to current ratio but often excludes inventory (also called Quick Ratio) |
| **Operating Cash Flow** | İşletme Faaliyetleri Nakit Akımı | Cash generated by the company's core business operations |

## The 15 Screening Criteria

### 1. Yıldız Pazar (Star Market)
**Meaning:** The stock must be listed on the **"Star Market"** segment of Borsa İstanbul (BIST), which is Turkey's stock exchange. The Star Market is the premium segment for larger, more established companies that meet higher listing standards.

**Implementation Note:** This is a categorical filter — filter stocks where `market_segment = "YILDIZ"` (or equivalent field in the data source).

---

### 2. Çeyreklikte Özkaynak Karlılığını Artıran (Quarterly Increasing Return on Equity)
**Meaning:** The company's **Return on Equity (ROE)** must be **increasing quarter over quarter**.

- **ROE** = Net Profit / Shareholders' Equity
- Compare the most recent quarter's ROE to the previous quarter's ROE.
- Condition: `ROE(Q_current) > ROE(Q_previous)`

**Why it matters:** Rising ROE means the company is becoming more efficient at generating profit from shareholder capital.

---

### 3. Çeyreklikte Net Karını Artıran (Quarterly Increasing Net Profit)
**Meaning:** The company's **Net Profit** must be **increasing quarter over quarter**.

- Condition: `NetProfit(Q_current) > NetProfit(Q_previous)`

**Why it matters:** Growing net profit is a basic sign of business health and momentum.

---

### 4. Çeyreklikte Satışlarını Artıran (Quarterly Increasing Sales/Revenue)
**Meaning:** The company's **Revenue (Sales)** must be **increasing quarter over quarter**.

- Condition: `Revenue(Q_current) > Revenue(Q_previous)`

**Why it matters:** Revenue growth indicates that the company is expanding its business, gaining customers, or increasing prices.

---

### 5. Çeyreklikte Favökünü Artıran (Quarterly Increasing EBITDA)
**Meaning:** The company's **EBITDA** must be **increasing quarter over quarter**.

- Condition: `EBITDA(Q_current) > EBITDA(Q_previous)`

**Why it matters:** EBITDA growth shows that the company's core operational profitability is improving, stripping out financing and accounting effects.

---

### 6. Çeyreklikte Favök Marjını Artıran (Quarterly Increasing EBITDA Margin)
**Meaning:** The company's **EBITDA Margin** must be **increasing quarter over quarter**.

- **EBITDA Margin** = EBITDA / Revenue
- Condition: `EBITDAMargin(Q_current) > EBITDAMargin(Q_previous)`

**Why it matters:** An improving EBITDA margin means the company is becoming more profitable relative to its revenue — it's scaling efficiently or controlling costs better.

---

### 7. Çeyreklikte Esas Faaliyet Karını Artıran (Quarterly Increasing Operating Profit)
**Meaning:** The company's **Operating Profit (EBIT)** must be **increasing quarter over quarter**.

- Condition: `OperatingProfit(Q_current) > OperatingProfit(Q_previous)`

**Why it matters:** Operating profit growth shows the core business is becoming more profitable, independent of tax strategies or debt structure.

---

### 8. Çeyreklikte Hisse Başına Karını Artıran (Quarterly Increasing EPS)
**Meaning:** The company's **Earnings Per Share (EPS)** must be **increasing quarter over quarter**.

- **EPS** = Net Profit / Total Shares Outstanding
- Condition: `EPS(Q_current) > EPS(Q_previous)`

**Why it matters:** Rising EPS means each share is generating more profit, which is directly positive for shareholders. It also accounts for share dilution.

---

### 9. Fiili Dolaşım Oranı %70'den Az (Free Float Rate Less Than 70%)
**Meaning:** The percentage of shares freely traded on the market must be **less than 70%**.

- Condition: `FreeFloatRate < 0.70`

**Why it matters:** A lower free float (more shares held by insiders/major shareholders) often indicates higher insider confidence and alignment of interests. It can also mean less selling pressure from institutional turnover. Very high free float can sometimes indicate weak insider commitment.

---

### 10. PEG Rasyosu 1'den Küçük (PEG Ratio Less Than 1)
**Meaning:** The **PEG Ratio** must be **less than 1**.

- **PEG** = (P/E Ratio) / Earnings Growth Rate
- Condition: `PEG < 1`

**Why it matters:** A PEG below 1 suggests the stock may be **undervalued relative to its growth rate**. It means you're paying less per unit of earnings growth, which is considered a value signal.

---

### 11. Cari Oran 1 ile 3 Arasında (Current Ratio Between 1 and 3)
**Meaning:** The **Current Ratio** must be **between 1 and 3**.

- **Current Ratio** = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- Condition: `1 <= CurrentRatio <= 3`

**Why it matters:**
- **Below 1**: The company may struggle to pay its short-term obligations (liquidity risk).
- **Above 3**: The company may be inefficiently using its assets (too much idle cash or inventory).
- **Between 1–3**: The sweet spot — healthy liquidity without wasteful asset hoarding.

---

### 12. Net Borç / Favök 3'den Küçük (Net Debt / EBITDA Less Than 3)
**Meaning:** The ratio of **Net Debt to EBITDA** must be **less than 3**.

- **Net Debt** = Total Debt - Cash & Cash Equivalents
- Condition: `NetDebt / EBITDA < 3`

**Why it matters:** This ratio shows how many years it would take the company to pay off its debt using its operating earnings. A value below 3 is generally considered manageable leverage. Above 3 signals potentially dangerous debt levels.

---

### 13. Nakit Oran 0.2'den Büyük (Cash Ratio Greater Than 0.2)
**Meaning:** The **Cash Ratio** must be **greater than 0.2**.

- **Cash Ratio** = Cash & Cash Equivalents / Current Liabilities
- Condition: `CashRatio > 0.20`

**Why it matters:** This is the strictest liquidity measure. It ensures the company has at least some cash cushion to cover immediate obligations. A cash ratio of 0.2 means for every 1 unit of short-term debt, the company holds at least 0.2 units in cash.

---

### 14. Likidite Oranı 0.5 ile 2 Arasında (Liquidity Ratio Between 0.5 and 2)
**Meaning:** The **Quick Ratio (Liquidity Ratio)** must be **between 0.5 and 2**.

- **Quick Ratio** = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
- Condition: `0.5 <= QuickRatio <= 2`

**Why it matters:** Similar to the current ratio but excludes inventory (which is less liquid). The range 0.5–2 avoids both illiquidity risk (too low) and excessive idle liquid assets (too high).

---

### 15. Yıllık İşletme Faaliyetleri Nakit Akım Değişimi 0'dan Büyük (Annual Operating Cash Flow Change Greater Than 0)
**Meaning:** The year-over-year **change in Operating Cash Flow** must be **positive**.

- Condition: `OperatingCashFlow(current_year) > OperatingCashFlow(previous_year)`

**Why it matters:** Operating cash flow is considered a more reliable indicator of financial health than net profit (which can be affected by accounting choices). A growing operating cash flow confirms that the company's profit is backed by real cash generation.

## Implementation Notes for TradingView Screener

TradingView's **Stock Screener** (and its **Pine Script** / **Screener API**) exposes many of these fields directly. Here is how they typically map:

| Our Field | TradingView Field Name (approximate) |
|-----------|--------------------------------------|
| Revenue | `total_revenue` |
| Net Profit | `net_income` |
| EBITDA | `ebitda` |
| Operating Profit | `operating_income` |
| EPS | `earnings_per_share_basic_ttm` |
| ROE | `return_on_equity` |
| Free Float | `float_shares_percent` |
| PEG Ratio | `price_earnings_to_growth_ttm` |
| Current Ratio | `current_ratio` |
| Net Debt / EBITDA | `net_debt_to_ebitda` |
| Cash Ratio | `cash_and_equivalents` / `total_current_liabilities` (may need manual calc) |
| Quick Ratio | `quick_ratio` |
| Operating Cash Flow | `cash_from_operations_annual` |

> **Note:** For the quarterly growth checks (criteria 2–8), TradingView may not provide a direct "QoQ growth" field. You may need to fetch the last 2 quarters of data and compute the comparison yourself, either via the TradingView API, or using a data provider like **Financial Modeling Prep**, **Polygon.io**, or **Yahoo Finance** as a supplement.

## Important Caveats

1. **Currency:** Since this screener targets Borsa İstanbul (BIST / Turkish market), all monetary values are in **Turkish Lira (TRY)**. Inflation effects in Turkey may distort nominal growth figures — this is worth discussing with your father.

2. **Quarterly vs. TTM:** Some TradingView fields use **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** rather than the most recent single quarter. Be sure to clarify which comparison period to use for the growth checks.

3. **EBITDA Margin computation:** TradingView may not expose EBITDA Margin directly — you may need to calculate it as `EBITDA / Revenue`.

4. **PEG Ratio edge cases:** PEG is undefined or misleading when the earnings growth rate is negative. You may want to add a guard: only check PEG when earnings growth rate > 0.

5. **Net Debt negativity:** If a company has more cash than debt, Net Debt is negative. `Net Debt / EBITDA` would then be negative too, which automatically passes the `< 3` check. Decide whether to explicitly handle this case.
